Key Takeaway

Molokhia's 28,000 Facebook followers exceed all Egyptian food competitors in Riyadh. However, the absence of a GBP and Snapchat presence, combined with a near-zero Facebook engagement rate, means competitors with smaller followings are generating significantly more orders per month through better platform distribution.

Competitive Gaps to Exploit First

01

Egyptian food on KSA TikTok is unclaimed

No Egyptian restaurant in Riyadh has a strong TikTok presence. 3 months of consistent posting can establish category ownership before any competitor moves.

02

Heritage storytelling is a content gap

Every competitor posts product photos. No brand is telling the cultural story of their food. A Heritage content series creates differentiation competitors cannot copy quickly.

03

Snapchat advertising is underpriced in KSA

Independent restaurants do not run Snapchat ads — leaving an underpriced channel to a young Riyadh food audience open for first-mover advantage.

04

Google Review floor is low

Most direct competitors have under 150 reviews. A 90-day review campaign targeting 60+ at 4.3+ stars would rank Molokhia first among Egyptian restaurants on Google Maps.
