05Website / Owned Presence Audit

No official website was discovered during this audit. Neither the Google Business Profile nor any social platform links to an owned site. The absence of a website results in:

  • Limited conversion control: Customers have no simple path to browse the menu, place an order or reserve a table directly.
  • No analytics or retargeting: Without a website, the business cannot track visitor behaviour, build audiences for ads, or collect first‑party data.
  • SEO limitations: A website with optimized content and schema could rank for local Egyptian‑restaurant queries and capture discovery traffic.
  • Brand authority: Owned media conveys professionalism and trust. Relying solely on social and third‑party platforms leaves the brand vulnerable to algorithm changes.

At minimum, the business should develop a responsive landing site with menu, ordering links, location map, contact details, and integration with Google Analytics 4.

07Highest‑Impact Recommendations

Quick wins (7 days)

  • Claim or update the Google Business Profile: correct the phone number, confirm operating hours and add a website placeholder (Linktree or WhatsApp order link).
  • Add a link in TikTok and Instagram bios (e.g., Linktree) directing visitors to order via phone, WhatsApp, or delivery apps.
  • Update the Facebook cover image with consistent phone number and include a call‑to‑action button (e.g., “Call Now” or “Order Now”).
  • Encourage staff to solicit Google reviews from recent happy customers to maintain recency and improve ranking.

Medium fixes (30 days)

  • Launch a minimal responsive website with menu, photos, story and order options. Integrate Google Analytics and Meta Pixel.
  • Standardize NAP across all platforms and directories (Google, Facebook, Yango, Instagram). Submit to additional local directories (TripAdvisor, Foursquare, Talabat).
  • Create a content calendar for TikTok and Instagram featuring a mix of offers, behind‑the‑scenes clips, chef spotlights, customer testimonials and educational posts about Egyptian cuisine.
  • Respond to all recent Google reviews — thanking positive reviewers and offering to address negative experiences offline.

Strategic improvements (90 days)

  • Develop a full‑funnel marketing strategy: awareness (educational content and influencer collaborations), consideration (menu highlights, cooking process), and conversion (limited‑time offers with direct order links).
  • Invest in geo‑targeted ads on Facebook, Instagram and TikTok to reach Egyptians and food enthusiasts in Riyadh.
  • Introduce a loyalty program and collect first‑party data through the website or WhatsApp ordering system.
  • Host live‑streamed cooking demonstrations and Q&A sessions to build community and differentiate from competitors.
  • Partner with delivery platforms to ensure menu accuracy, quality photos and promo campaigns; negotiate featured placement during Ramadan and other peak seasons.
08Content Strategy Diagnosis

Weaknesses: Based on available evidence, Molokhia’s content appears heavily offer‑centric. Without access to posts, it is likely dominated by platters and price promotions. Captions may be short and direct, lacking storytelling or sensory description. There is little evidence of human‑centric content.

Over‑posting: Frequent promotion of offers without variety can fatigue the audience and commoditise the brand.

Under‑posting: Few behind‑the‑scenes videos, staff introductions, customer testimonials, cultural stories or educational posts about Egyptian cuisine.

Missing pillars: Ambience & family experience, chef & kitchen stories, ingredient sourcing & quality, Egyptian culture & traditions and customer success stories. Introducing these pillars would create an emotional connection and differentiate the restaurant.

Recommended style: Use warm, cinematic lighting and close‑up shots of dishes. Incorporate Arabic calligraphy and subtle Riyadh references. Mix short videos (recipes, plating), carousels of menu items and user‑generated content re‑shares. Feature Egyptian music and local slang to connect with the Riyadh audience.

09Sales / Conversion Diagnosis

Conversion Friction:

  • No direct link from social profiles to order or reserve, forcing users to search manually.
  • Multiple phone numbers create confusion and risk missed calls.
  • Missing website means there is no unified ordering funnel or ability to retarget visitors with ads.
  • Google listing lacks call‑to‑action buttons and menu links, reducing conversions from Maps searches.

Proof & Trust Issues:

  • Few photos and no menu on GBP diminish visitor confidence when deciding where to dine.
  • Lack of responses to reviews may make potential customers question service quality.
  • Not showcasing hygiene practices or staff training leaves safety perceptions to chance.

Recommended Solutions:

  • Add order/reservation links to all bios and the GBP. Use a unified WhatsApp Business link for quick orders.
  • Produce proof‑driven content: customer testimonials, influencer visits, ratings highlights and awards.
  • Ensure the menu is easily accessible via the website and Google profile. Encourage reviews after each transaction.

  • What percentage of current orders come from phone calls versus delivery platforms?
  • Have you claimed and verified your Google Business Profile? Who manages updates and responses?
  • How often do you post on TikTok and Instagram, and which content performs best?
  • Are you using any paid advertising on Meta, TikTok or Google? What results have you seen?
  • Do you track how customers hear about you (walk‑in vs. social vs. referrals)?
  • What systems do you use for managing customer feedback and reviews?
  • Is there an existing database of customer contacts (e.g., WhatsApp, email) for promotions?
  • What distinguishes Molokhia from other Egyptian restaurants in Riyadh, and how is that communicated?
  • Do you have seasonal or holiday offers (e.g., Ramadan) planned in advance?
  • What technology platforms do you use for ordering, delivery and reservations?

Probable objections and responses

  • “We already have customers; why invest in a website?” — A website gives you ownership of your audience, improves SEO and conversion rates and provides analytics to grow further.
  • “Our posts get likes, so content is fine.” — Engagement is a vanity metric. Without a strategic mix of brand, educational and conversion content, likes don’t translate into repeat visits or orders.
  • “It’s hard to manage reviews across platforms.” — Tools and simple processes can automate review prompts and responses. Responding builds trust and improves ranking.
  • “Paid ads are expensive.” — Local geo‑targeted ads on Meta and TikTok can start with small budgets and yield measurable results, especially when combined with organic content.
  • “Our phone number is everywhere; people will find us.” — Inconsistent NAP across platforms confuses both customers and search engines, leading to lost calls and lower rankings.
